Press a key. Type the thought. Back to work.

Crane is a capture bar for macOS. Hit ⌘⇧Space anywhere, type, press ↵ — about two seconds, and you never left your editor. Every entry is tagged as it lands by Apple’s Foundation Models, running entirely on your Mac. No cloud, no account, nothing leaves the machine.

Nothing leaves your Mac. Not even for the AI.

Most “smart” capture tools ship your half-formed thoughts to someone else’s server. Crane doesn’t have a server.

On-device intelligence

Tagging runs on Apple’s Foundation Models — the system models behind Apple Intelligence, executing on your Mac’s own silicon. No API key, no account, no quota, works offline.

Local by architecture

Crane makes zero network requests. Your notes live in one local file — ~/Library/Application Support/com.abhaycs.crane — yours to back up, inspect, or delete.

Native, not wrapped

Swift and SwiftUI, a real menu-bar app — no Electron, no Dock icon, no telemetry. It waits quietly until you press the key.
